Why Price Per mg Is the Only Number That Matters

When you're comparing peptide vendors, the question isn't "how much does this vial cost?" It's "how much am I paying for each milligram of active compound?"

Two vials can be priced identically but contain very different amounts. Our comparison table normalizes everything to cost per mg so you can make an apples-to-apples comparison instantly.

What to Look for Beyond Price

Cost per mg is the starting point, but there are a few other factors worth weighing:

Available discounts — Several vendors on our platform accept affiliate codes that reduce the final price. We list the active code for each vendor and the discount percentage directly in the table. Always check whether a discount applies before buying.

Vial size options — Some vendors offer multiple sizes. Buying larger quantities typically reduces cost per mg. If you're doing a longer research protocol, it's worth calculating whether a larger vial makes sense economically.

In-stock status — Popular compounds go out of stock. If your preferred vendor is out, check the table for the next best option that meets the 7/7 standard.

Shipping speed — Not listed in the table but worth noting: most vendors ship within 1–3 business days. Check each vendor's current processing times before ordering.

How We Update Prices

Prices in our comparison table are updated weekly. Research peptide pricing shifts frequently — vendors run sales, adjust batch pricing, and occasionally change their standard rates.

We check every vendor's current pricing every week and update the table manually. If you notice a discrepancy, use the contact link at the bottom of the page to flag it.

The Bottom Line

BPC-157 is widely available across quality vendors right now. The price gap between the best and worst deals is meaningful — often 20–30% when you factor in discount codes and price per mg. Spend 60 seconds in the comparison table before placing an order and you'll almost always find a better deal than your first instinct.
