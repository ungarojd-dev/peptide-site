What Is the 7/7 Testing Standard for Research Peptides?

📅 January 20, 2026 ✍️ MyPeptidePrice Research Team ⏱ 6 min read 🔄 Updated 2026
Quick Answer

The 7/7 testing standard requires research peptide vendors to provide independent lab results across 7 categories: net purity, net content, heavy metals, endotoxins, identification, sterility, and conformity. Vendors who only publish a purity result are passing 1 of 7, not the full standard.

What Is the 7/7 Testing Standard?

The 7/7 testing standard is a quality benchmark used to evaluate research peptide vendors. A vendor meets the standard only when independent third-party labs confirm their products pass all seven of the following tests, conducted by labs with no financial relationship to the vendor.

  1. Net Purity, Confirms the percentage of active compound. A vial labeled 99% pure should contain at least 99% of the target peptide.
  2. Net Content, Confirms the actual milligram amount in the vial matches what is advertised.
  3. Heavy Metals, Screens for toxic trace metals including lead, arsenic, mercury, and cadmium.
  4. Endotoxins, Tests for bacterial endotoxins that are invisible to purity tests.
  5. Identification, Confirms the compound is actually the peptide it claims to be.
  6. Sterility, Confirms the product is free from viable microbial contamination.
  7. Conformity, Confirms the product's lot number and specifications match the published Certificate of Analysis.

Why Do Most Peptide Vendors Only Run 1 or 2 Tests?

HPLC purity testing costs relatively little and produces a clean, marketable result. Running all seven tests adds significant cost per batch. Most vendors publish a single purity number and call it a "full CoA", but a purity result cannot detect heavy metals, endotoxins, or whether a vial contains the advertised amount.

How Do You Read a Peptide Certificate of Analysis?

When reviewing any vendor's CoA, check three things:

  • Who ran the test? The lab name should be a recognized independent testing facility, not "in-house" or the vendor's own name.
  • How old is it? Results older than 12 months for the current batch are a red flag. Each new production run should have its own current CoA.
  • Are all 7 categories covered? If you see only "Purity: 99.1%" with no mention of heavy metals, endotoxins, or sterility, the CoA is incomplete.

What Is the Difference Between Purity and Identity Testing?

Purity testing tells you how much of the sample is the target compound. Identity testing confirms which compound it actually is. Both are required for a complete quality picture. A high purity result on the wrong compound still means the wrong product was received.

Does Price Per mg Change When Factoring in Testing Quality?

Yes. A vial that is 65% pure costs more per effective milligram than a fully tested 99% pure vial at a higher list price. Real cost per mg = price ÷ (mg × purity%). Frequently Asked Questions

What is the 7/7 testing standard for research peptides?

The 7/7 standard requires vendors to provide independent lab results for seven tests: net purity, net content, heavy metals, endotoxins, identification, sterility, and conformity. All seven must be performed by a third-party lab. All products are for laboratory research use only.

Why do most peptide vendors only test for purity?

HPLC purity testing is the cheapest option available. Running all seven tests costs significantly more per batch, so vendors who prioritize margins over transparency typically skip the remaining six tests.

How do I read a peptide Certificate of Analysis?

Check: (1) the lab name, must be a named third party; (2) the date, within 12 months; (3) coverage, all 7 test categories listed. A CoA showing only purity is incomplete.
